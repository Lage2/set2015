Susana Sargento

Co-Founder of Veniam

Susana Sargento finished her Doctor's Degree in Elctronical Engineering in 2003 at Universidade de Aveiro. She was a professor of the Department of Computer Sciences at Universidade do Porto from September 2002 to February 2004, and since then she has worked at Universidade de Aveiro and Instituto de Telecomunicações where she currently leads the team of Network Arquitecture and Protocol (http://nap.av.it.pt). Susana has also co-founded Veniam, a startup from Universidade de Aveiro e do Porto that comercializes vehicular technology.

She possesses 15 years of experience in technical leadership from multiple national and international projects, and she has worked in close collaboration with comunications operators and OEMs. She has recently been involved with several FP7 projects (4WARD, Euro-NF, C-Cast, WIP, Daidalos, C-Mobile), EU Coordinated Support Action 2012-316296 "FUTURE-CITIES", as well as national and CMU|Portugal projects (DRIVEIN with the Carnegie Melon University).

For instance, on the IST-Daidalos project, a 30M € project, she lead two main activities (QoS e redes ad-hoc). On the FUTURE-CITIES project, she is responsible for the implementation of the vehicular network on busses and trucks, as well as several fixed stations, making it over 600 vehicles in Porto. Susana is mainly known for her successes in QoS and IP mobility, network virtualization and Cloud resources, and distribuited automanagement, as well as vehicular protocol and mechanisms and ad-hoc.
